@Kowloon Walled City Perimeter  - NIGHT (NOVEMBER 1992)
@Wong reaches into the inner breast pocket of her black leather jacket. She pulls out a black leather book-wallet and flips it open. The colonial RHKPF crest — the St. Edward's Crown over the harbour scene — gleams under the flickering neon.
She slots the back flap of the wallet securely over the waistband of her jeans. The black-and-white photo ID sits exposed right next to her holstered revolver.
@Wong: Drop the colonial theatrics, Deedman. PTU has the perimeter locked down. If we go into the Dark City without our cards showing, our own tactical units will put a bullet in us before the Triads do.
@Deedman: (looking at the towering, windowless monolith of concrete) My informant swore the ex-detective is hiding in a fake dentist clinic on the third level. But my Cantonese isn't up to this grid. I can't read the alley markers.
@Wong draws a heavy metal flashlight from her jacket pocket. She snaps the beam onward, cutting through the damp, smoky air of the entrance.
@Wong: Nobody can read them, Deedman. For a hundred years, this place has been "Mo Wong Goon" — outside the King's control. Your British law doesn't mean a damn thing past that doorway.
A sudden, sharp voice echoes from a dark upper corridor deep inside the maze.
TRIAD VOICE (V.O.): (distant, shouting in Cantonese) Tsai-Goa! Tsai-Goa lai lo! (Cops! The cops are here!)
@Wong smiles faintly, adjusting the collar of her leather jacket against the chilly autumn draft blowing out from the concrete labyrinth.
@Wong: See? They already know we're here. Stay three paces behind me, keep your hand on your holster, and let me do the talking. Let's go hunt a ghost.
